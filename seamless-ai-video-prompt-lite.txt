DIALOGUE PERFORMANCE LAYER (TOKEN EFFICIENT)

Applies only when dialogue exists in a clip.

When dialogue is present:

- Specify emotion
- Specify tone of voice
- Specify vocal intensity
- Specify delivery style (whispered, strained, breathless, controlled, cold, trembling, clipped, etc.)
- Keep descriptors concise (2–6 words per attribute max)
- Do not over-describe unless emotion shifts mid-line

Format inside the Dialogue section:

Character Name (emotion, tone, intensity, delivery):
"Dialogue line"

If emotion shifts mid-line:

Character Name (emotion → emotion shift, tone consistent):
"Dialogue line"

If multiple characters speak:
Apply performance tags to each speaker individually.

If no dialogue:
Do not include performance tags.

VISIBILITY & NATURAL BEHAVIOR CONTINUITY — CONTINUOUS MODE

This rule enforces realistic continuity without robotic staging.

END-FRAME REQUIREMENT:
- Any subject, body part, prop, device, environmental element, or light source
  that will be visible OR used in the NEXT clip
  MUST be visible in the FINAL FRAME of the CURRENT clip.

MID-CLIP FLEXIBILITY:
- Elements may temporarily leave frame mid-clip.
- Body parts may move naturally out of frame.
- Props may be briefly occluded.
- Natural motion is encouraged.
- HOWEVER: If required next clip, the element MUST be visible again
  by the final frame of the current clip.

NO FORCED STAGING:
- Subjects must NEVER hold unnatural poses solely for continuity.
- No “Superman arms out” positioning.
- No rigid body locking.
- No frozen gestures to preserve visibility.
- Natural human behavior ALWAYS overrides mechanical staging.

IDENTITY PERSISTENCE ACROSS FULL SEQUENCE:
- Any wearable item (example: wristwatch, ring, jacket),
  hairstyle, facial hair, makeup, damage, dirt, sweat,
  or defining feature introduced in Clip 1
  must remain identical across ALL clips
  unless explicitly removed within the narrative.

- If a wristwatch exists in Clip 1,
  it must be the SAME wristwatch in all later clips.
- No silent swaps.
- No design drift.
- No color shifts.
- No spontaneous appearance/disappearance.

OFF-SCREEN PRESENCE RULE:
- Off-screen implied presence does NOT count.
- Visibility must be literal and on-frame at end if needed next clip.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TEMPORAL CONSISTENCY

- Motion must continue smoothly from previous clip's final frame
- No sudden position jumps
- Ongoing actions must complete naturally
- Momentum must follow physical realism
- Micro-movements (breathing, blinking, weight shifts) encouraged

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SCENE COMPOSITION LOCK — CRITICAL

- Clip 1 defines all visible elements (STATIC mode)
- From Clip 2 onward (STATIC mode):
  - NO new objects may appear
  - NO new subjects may enter
  - NO objects may disappear unless visibly removed earlier
  - Camera may not reveal undisclosed areas

Allowed:
- Natural repositioning
- Temporary occlusion
- Lighting interaction changes
- Emotional expression shifts
- Realistic body language changes

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FINAL FRAME REQUIREMENTS

- Every clip must end on a stable, usable frame
- Final frame must be fully lit and readable
- No fades to black
- No fade-outs
- No white flashes
- No cinematic dimming
- Final frame must intentionally stage elements required for next clip
- Staging must appear natural and motivated
