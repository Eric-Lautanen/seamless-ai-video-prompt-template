CAMERA MOVEMENT TERMINOLOGY (UNIVERSAL):
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

**LINEAR MOVEMENTS:**
- Dolly/Track: Forward, backward (measured in ft/sec or m/sec)
- Truck: Left, right lateral movement
- Pedestal/Crane: Up, down vertical movement

**ROTATIONAL MOVEMENTS:**
- Pan: Horizontal rotation (measured in degrees/sec)
- Tilt: Vertical rotation (degrees/sec)
- Roll/Dutch: Axis rotation for horizon tilt

**COMPLEX MOVEMENTS:**
- Arc: Circular path around subject (radius + degrees/sec)
- Orbital: Complete 360° circle around subject
- Boom: Combined crane + dolly
- Jib: Sweeping arc with altitude change
- Steadicam/Handheld: Organic float with controlled shake (specify intensity)

**LENS MOVEMENTS:**
- Zoom: In/out (measured in focal length change or magnification multiplier)
- Rack Focus: Shift focus plane (specify depth range)
- Crash Zoom: Rapid zoom (speed in seconds to complete)

**SPEED DESCRIPTORS:**
- Glacial: 0.1-0.3 ft/sec or 1-3°/sec
- Slow: 0.5-1 ft/sec or 5-10°/sec
- Moderate: 1.5-3 ft/sec or 15-30°/sec
- Fast: 4-6 ft/sec or 40-60°/sec
- Rapid: 7+ ft/sec or 70+°/sec

When creating sequences, remember these content-specific priorities:

**NARRATIVE/CHARACTER:**
- Eye-line and gaze direction must be tracked
- Character interaction distances and formations
- Performance continuity (mid-word, mid-gesture states)
- Screen direction rules (characters shouldn't flip sides unintentionally)

**DIALOGUE/CONVERSATION:**
- 180° line/axis of action (don't cross unless intentional)
- Eye-line matching between characters (angles must correspond)
- Shot size progression (wide → medium → close typically)
- Performance timing (who's speaking, who's reacting, pauses)
- Over-shoulder framing (how much of foreground character visible)
- Background/foreground character sharpness relationship

**DANCE/CHOREOGRAPHY:**
- Movement phrase completion (don't cut mid-phrase unless intentional)
- Spatial relationships between dancers (formations, partnering)
- Synchronization timing (unison, canon, syncopated)
- Level changes (floor, standing, jumps, lifts)
- Quality of movement (sustained, percussive, fluid, sharp)
- Stage geography (upstage, downstage, stage-left/right)
- Camera should complement choreography, not fight it

**WILDLIFE/NATURE:**
- Subject behavior predictability (flight path vs erratic movement)
- Environmental interaction (disturbing foliage, water)
- Respect for natural movement patterns
- Seasonal/time-of-day lighting consistency

**ACTION/DYNAMIC:**
- Speed ramping timing (when to slow-mo, when real-time)
- Impact anticipation and follow-through
- Handheld shake intensity levels
- Motion blur appropriate to speed

**AERIAL/DRONE:**
- Altitude tracking (AGL measurements)
- Gimbal angle progression
- Wind effect simulation (subtle drift)
- Reveal geography (what's visible at each altitude)

**TECHNICAL/PRODUCT:**
- Macro depth of field critical zones
- Reflection and highlight control
- Surface texture visibility requirements
- Focus tracking precision

**VFX/FANTASY:**
- Practical vs CG element interaction
- Effect progression states (0% to 100% manifestation)
- Volumetric lighting from CG sources
- Compositing blend notes

QUICK REFERENCE: COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

❌ **DON'T:**
- Use approximations: "~15%", "about 3 feet", "roughly centered"
- Omit fields: Skipping Focus/DOF or Background State because "it hasn't changed"
- Use vague transitions: "Then the camera moves to the bridge"
- Use shorthand for START FRAME: "[match previous]", "[same as Clip X]", or "[Assumed continuity...]" - ALWAYS copy verbatim
- Use shorthand even in abbreviated outputs: If showing only Clips 1, 5, 10, still provide full START FRAMES for each
- Forget START FRAME: Clip 2+ must always have START FRAME matching previous END FRAME
- Mix measurement systems: Use consistent ft/sec OR m/sec, not both
- Ignore motion blur progression: Even 0% should be stated explicitly
- Vague VFX descriptions: "particles increase" without numbers

✅ **DO:**
- Use exact measurements: "15% frame height", "3 feet", "centered at 50% horizontal"
- Fill every field every time: State "Maintaining f/8 deep focus" if unchanged
- Specify smooth blended transitions: "Continue dolly 2 sec, introduce pan while maintaining dolly 2 sec, end dolly over final 2 sec"
- Copy END FRAME verbatim to next START FRAME: Full text, word-for-word, no shortcuts
- Provide complete START FRAMES even in abbreviated outputs: If showing Clip 10 without Clip 9, reconstruct Clip 9's END FRAME from context and copy it verbatim to Clip 10's START FRAME
- State "No change" or "Maintaining previous" rather than omitting
- Track motion blur changes: "0% → 5% → 8% → 5% → 0%" progression
- Quantify VFX elements: "Spacetime distortion: 15 ripple waves at 35% opacity → 42 waves at 68% opacity, wave radius: 25% frame → 50% frame, propagation speed: 10 ft/sec"
