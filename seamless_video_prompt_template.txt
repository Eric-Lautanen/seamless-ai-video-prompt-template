CAMERA MOVEMENT RULES — CRITICAL

CONTINUITY REQUIREMENTS:
- Camera movement must be explicitly stated for EVERY clip
- If no movement intended, state "camera remains completely static"
- Camera position from previous clip's end = this clip's start
- Impossible movements (sudden position jumps) forbidden unless using CUTS mode

MOVEMENT SPECIFICATIONS MUST INCLUDE:
- Type: pan, tilt, dolly, truck, pedestal, zoom, orbit, static
- Direction: left, right, up, down, in, out, clockwise, counterclockwise
- Speed: slow, medium, fast, or specific (e.g., "2 degrees per second")
- Arc or path if relevant (e.g., "smooth 45-degree arc")

SPATIAL CONTINUITY RULES:
- 180-DEGREE RULE: Camera must stay on one side of the action axis
  - Draw imaginary line between two subjects
  - All camera positions must remain on same side of this line
  - Crossing the line causes spatial disorientation (forbidden unless motivated cut)
  - Exception: Motivated crossing via camera movement shown on screen
  
- 30-DEGREE RULE: When cutting to same subject from different angle:
  - Camera angle must change by at least 30 degrees
  - Less than 30 degrees creates jarring "jump cut" effect
  - Exception: Jump cuts allowed if intentional for stylistic effect
  
- EYELINE MATCH: When characters look at each other:
  - Subject A looks frame-right → Subject B must look frame-left
  - Eye direction must remain consistent across clips
  - Looking off-screen must be matched with corresponding reverse angle
  
- SCREEN DIRECTION: Movement direction must remain consistent:
  - If subject moves left-to-right in Clip 1, maintain this in Clip 2
  - Exception: Show the turn on screen if direction changes

FORBIDDEN UNLESS EXPLICIT:
- Sudden reframing between clips
- Unintended drift or shake
- Random zoom in/out
- Camera "finding" better composition
- Auto-correction of framing
- Crossing the 180-degree line without motivation
- Jump cuts under 30 degrees (unless stylistic)

CONSISTENCY LOCKS:
- If Clip 1 uses handheld style, all clips must maintain this
- If Clip 1 uses steady/locked-off camera, maintain throughout
- Focal length must remain constant unless zoom is explicit
- Camera height must remain constant unless pedestal movement specified

CAMERA MOTION PHYSICS (GLOBAL):
• ONE primary camera move per sequence unless editorially justified
• Linear acceleration ≤ 2 ft/sec²
• Rotational acceleration ≤ 5°/sec²
• Motion changes must blend over 1–2 seconds using easing curves
• Camera inertia defined per clip (0–1)
• Damping required for rotation and dolly (0–1)

MOTION BLUR SCALE:
0–5%    Minimal
5–15%   Subtle
15–25%  Moderate
25–40%  Strong
40%+    Extreme (cuts only)
